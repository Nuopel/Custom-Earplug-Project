# LOT 1 Report — Preparation & Validation Protocol

## 1.1 Purpose

Define and validate a **safe, repeatable and documented protocol** for ear impressions, including material selection and preparation steps.

This lot **de-risks the entire project**.

**Safety disclaimer.**  
Although this first trial demonstrates the practical feasibility of the molding procedure, ear-impression taking remains a potentially risky operation when performed without appropriate training, materials, or precautions. Any attempt to reproduce this procedure is done entirely at the user’s own risk. For safety reasons, ear impressions should preferably be taken by a qualified professional such as an audiologist or hearing-care specialist.

### 1.3.1 Research Biocompatible Materials and Required Tools

**Goal:** Select at least one suitable impression silicone.

**Output:** Material comparison table.

The first silicone reviewed was the ABR Ear Impression Putty Silicone product. It is a two-part **ear impression putty** (200 g base + 200 g catalyst) mixed at a 1:1 ratio that sets in about 3 to 5 minutes to form an accurate, stable, and slightly soft mold (37 Shore A +/- 2) of the ear canal and outer ear.

The second silicone product was a **two-part translucent liquid silicone mold-making kit** (Part A + Part B, mixed 1:1) designed for creating flexible, high-detail molds for resin casting, candles, soap, wax, and other DIY projects. The silicone is odorless, non-toxic, eco-friendly, and tear-resistant, cures at room temperature in about 24 hours, and releases easily from finished pieces, producing smooth, bubble-free molds that can be tinted with mica powder for custom colors.

Additional required items include the syringe used to inject the material into the ear canal and the Ear Impression Pads Otoblock used to protect the eardrum.

### 1.3.2 Define Safety Protocol

**Goal:** Prevent injury, discomfort, or blockage.

To ensure safe and reliable ear impression taking, a dedicated safety protocol has been established to prevent any risk of injury, discomfort, infection, or material blockage. The protocol formalizes good practice before, during, and after the impression process and serves as a reference for operators and users. A complete operational checklist is provided in the associated Safety Protocol document.

**Main safety steps:**

1. **Pre-checks before procedure**
   
   - Verify absence of ear pain, infection, inflammation, or perforated eardrum
   
   - Inspect ear canal using an otoscope
   
   - Ensure ear canal is clean and free of excessive earwax

2. **Physical protection of the ear**
   
   - Insert an otoblock (cotton or foam dam) under visual control
   
   - Position the dam beyond the second bend of the ear canal
   
   - Control insertion depth to avoid contact with the eardrum

3. **Hygiene and personal protective equipment (PPE)**
   
   - Wear disposable gloves
   
   - Use disposable mixing and injection tips
   
   - Work on a clean and disinfected workspace

4. **Safe material injection and curing**
   
   - Inject silicone impression material slowly and without pressure
   
   - Monitor user comfort continuously
   
   - Maintain immobility during curing phase

5. **Emergency and abnormal situation management**
   
   - Stop procedure immediately in case of pain or abnormal sensation
   
   - Avoid forceful extraction if material is stuck
   
   - Refer to medical professional when necessary

This structured sequence ensures repeatability, operator safety, and user protection, and forms the basis of the detailed Safety Protocol checklist presented in the dedicated protocol section.
